Automated Bone Cancer Detection
Using Deep Learning on X-Ray Images
Sasanka Sekhar Dalai, PhD1, Bharat Jyoti Ranjan Sahu, PhD1,
Jyotirmayee Rautaray, PhD 1, M. Ijaz Khan, PhD 2,
Bander A. Jabr, PhD 3, and Yasser A. Ali, PhD 3
Abstract
In recent days, bone cancer is a life-threatening health issue that can lead to death. However, physicians use CT-scan,
X-rays, or MRI images to recognize bone cancer, but still require techniques to increase precision and reduce human
labor. These methods face challenges such as high costs, time consumption, and the risk of misdiagnosis due to the
complexity of bone tumor appearances. Therefore, it is essential to establish an automated system to detect healthy
bones from cancerous ones. In this regard, Arti ﬁcial intelligence, particularly deep learning, shows increased attention in
the medical image analysis process. This research presents a new Golden Search Optimization along with Deep Learning
Enabled Computer Aided Diagnosis for Bone Cancer Classiﬁcation (GSODL-CADBCC) on X-ray images. The aim of the
GSODL-CADBCC approach is to accurately distinguish the input X-ray images into healthy and cancerous. This re-
search presents the GSODL-CADBCC technique that leverages the bilateral ﬁltering technique to remove the noise.
This method uses the SqueezeNet model to generate feature vectors, and the GSO algorithm ef ﬁciently selects the
hyperparameters. Finally, the extracted features can be classi ﬁed by improved cuckoo search with a long short-term
memory model. The experimental results demonstrate that the GSODL- CADBCC approach attains highest per-
formance with an average accuracy of 95.52% on the training set data and 94.79% on the testing set data. This automated
approach not only reduces the need for manual interpretation but also minimizes the risk of diagnostic errors and
provides a viable option for precise medical imaging-based bone cancer screening.

computer aided diagnosis, golden search optimization, medical imaging, deep learning, bone cancer

The Proposed GSODL-CADBCC Model
In this research, an effective GSODL-CADBCC approach
was introduced for automated bone cancer classi ﬁcation
on X-ray images. The presented GSODL-CADBCC
technique encompasses BF with noise removal, GSO
along with SqueezeNet consisting of feature extraction,
LSTM classi ﬁcation, and ICS related hyperparameter

Image Preprocessing Using BF Technique
Firstly, the GSODL-CADBCC technique annihilate the
noise in the X-ray images with the help of BF technique.
The BF algorithm is employed in the GSODL-CADBCC
model to preprocess X-ray images by reducing noise
while preserving important structural details like edges.
This step is crucial because medical images, such as
X-rays, often contain noise that can obscure critical
features needed for accurate diagnosis. BF effectively
removes noise from images, which is essential for en-
hancing the clarity and quality of medical images. Noise
can signi ﬁcantly affect the performance of feature ex-
traction and classi ﬁcation algorithms. Unlike traditional
smoothing techniques that blur edges, BF maintains sharp
edges by considering both the spatial closeness and the
intensity difference of pixels. This property is particularly
important in medical imaging, where edges delineate
anatomical structures and regions of interest. BF algo-
rithm is a nonlinear ﬁltering technique that completely
considers spatial and pixel value information and lastly
forms a compromise processing on the image.
18 In
medical images, especially in X-rays, the regions of in-
terest (such as bones and tumors) tend to be narrow and
elongated compared to random noise, which is usually
more dispersed and lacks structure. This characteristic
allows BF to differentiate between true edges and noise.

Bone Cancer Classi ﬁcation
For automated recognition of bone cancer, the LSTM
model is used. LSTM is an innovative structure in RNN
that is frequently employed to handle time series tasks.
Traditional RNNs suffer from the vanishing gradient
problem, where gradients become too small for effective
learning during backpropagation. LSTMs mitigate this
issue through their gating mechanisms which enable the
network to learn and propagate gradients over long dis-
tances. In comparison to conventional FFNN, LSTM has
memory ability and allows the data stream to continue to
ﬂow inside the network.
21 It is capable of linking the prior
data to the existing problem. However, the past status data
is useful for deciding the present state of equipment. In
medical imaging, especially for tasks like bone cancer
detection, it is crucial to capture the sequential nature of
the image slices. Each slice of an X-ray can be considered
a step in a sequence, and LSTMs are adept at learning
from these sequences. LSTM has four major components,
viz, output gate, input gate, forget gate, and cell state. The
data of the cell state is updated by using three gates that
automatically make LSTM add or remove data to the
neural network. LSTMs are capable of learning complex
patterns and dependencies in the data, which improves
classiﬁcation accuracy. With respect to bone cancer de-
tection, the model can better distinguish between healthy
and cancerous bone structures by leveraging temporal
information and dependencies within the image slices.
LSTMs can retain information over long sequences,
which is bene ﬁcial for medical image analysis where the
context from previous slices can provide essential clues
for accurate diagnosis. LSTMs maintain a cell state that
can preserve important information across many time
steps. The use of input, output, and forget gates allows the
network to decide which information to keep and which to
discard enhances the model ’s ability to focus on relevant
features. Forget gate decides how much data is forgotten

Finally, the ICS algorithm ﬁne-tunes the hyper-

accuracy. CS is derived from the lifestyle of a bird family

motivation for the improvement of new evolutionary
optimization techniques. 22 CS begins with an early
population that has matured cuckoo and their eggs. Every
matured cuckoo has its egg laying radius (ELR) and lays
eggs in its ELR in host bird habitat. Next, some eggs that
are less like host bird eggs will be discarded. The original
CS algorithm can address various optimization challenges
due to its exploration and exploitation capabilities.
However, to improve its performance for speci ﬁc prob-
lems, some modi ﬁcations are necessary. The original CS
algorithm efﬁciently balances exploration (searching new
areas) and exploitation (re ﬁning existing solutions).
However, for complex or speci ﬁc optimization problems,
this balance might need adjustment. The performance of
the CS algorithm depends on parameters like step size and
probability of discovery.
Survived cuckoo population will be migrated to the
best location. The ﬁrst cuckoo based on the location in
environments is separated into some clusters and lastly,
every cuckoo will be migrated towards the better cuckoo
in the better cluster with λ percentage and with the de-
viation of α radians. Once each position of the cuckoo
became closer, the process ends. Being closer means that
this environment has maximum food source and fewer
eggs will demise. But the original CS is capable of re-
solving a number of challenges, by shifting some of its
operations, and based on the problems, we could enhance
its overall performance. In the proposed model, local
search technique is added termed “Simulated Annealing ”
and few operations of CS are to enhance it. The outcomes
demonstrated that the modi ﬁcation employed on the CS
enhanced the over zperformance. To enhance the overall
performance of the CS algorithm, we propose several
modiﬁcations: adaptively decreasing the Egg egg-laying
radius over iterations, adjusting the number of eggs
based on ﬁtness, and implementing a repair mechanism
for out-of-bound solutions. Additionally, integrating
local search with Simulated Annealing to re ﬁne the best
solutions and using clustering and migration to leverage
collective intelligence. These enhancements improve
exploration and exploitation balance, ensure feasible
solutions, and accelerate convergence, making the ICS
more effective for complex optimization problems in
applications like the GSODL-CADBCC model. The
changes are given below:
Adaptive ELR. At the highest iteration, the estimated so-
lution should be altered less than the prior iteration, and
the size of ELR should be decreased. It shows that eggs
aren’t laid farther away from the parents. In the presented
method, the size of ELR is decreased while the amount of
iterations is improved.

cuckoo is randomly de ﬁned, between the minimum and
the maximum number of eggs. In the presented method, it
is potential that a weaker cuckoo might lay more eggs
when compared to the stronger one. This might decrease

Conclusion
In this work, an effective GSODL-CADBCC approach
was introduced for automated bone cancer classi ﬁcation
on X-ray images. The presented GSODL-CADBCC
technique encompasses BF based noise removal, GSO
with SqueezeNet based feature extraction, LSTM clas-
siﬁcation, and ICS based hyperparameter tuning. The
design of GSO and ICS algorithms helps in the optimal
selection of the hyper-parameters of the SqueezeNet and
LSTM models. An extensive set of experiments was
conducted to study the performance of the GSODL-
CADBCC method on medical imaging datasets. Com-
pared with recent DL models, the experimental outcomes
demonstrated that the GSODL-CADBCC technique
achieves promising performance on bone cancer classi-
ﬁcation. Therefore, the GSODL-CADBCC technique can
be exploited for automated and accurate bone cancer
classiﬁcation. In the future, deep instance segmentation
techniques can be included in the GSODL-CADBCC
technique to improve its classi ﬁcation ef ﬁcacy.
